Pizza

This pizza comes from the website, allrecipes.com and was originally given by Lynda Q. Some pizzas have a thick crust, but this one has a thin crust ideal for those out there that want to enjoy pizza but have braces or more brittle teeth that may not be able to handle thicker crusts without issues. This pizza will very well be scrumptious as a result of that.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • Quarter teaspoon of white sugar
  • Three-Fourths cup of Lukewarm water
  • 2 cups of all purpose flour, divided
  • Half teaspoon salt
  1. Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water that's 110 degrees F (44 degrees C) in a small bowl. Let stand until yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam, about 5 to 8 minutes.
  2. Combine 1 3/4 cups flour and salt in a large bowl. Pour in yeast mixture; mix well until dough comes together.
  3. Transfer dough to a floured surface and knead until smooth, about 2 minutes. Add remaining 1/4 cup flour if dough is too sticky.
  4. Roll dough into a 12-inch circle. Transfer to a greased pizza pan.
  5. Top crust as desired and bake at 500 degrees F (260 degrees C) until golden, 8 to 10 minutes.
